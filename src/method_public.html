How the forecast model works

1 · Data preparation

Each record is one plaza-month with transaction counts and revenue by vehicle class. Duplicate plaza-month rows (split source records) were summed — transactions are additive. Passenger = Car/Jeep/Van; Commercial = LCV + Bus/Truck + 3-Axle + 4-6 Axle + OSV.

2 · Model form

Every series (network, state, plaza; revenue or traffic; total, passenger or commercial) is fit with the same structural time-series model — the decomposition Facebook Prophet uses for monthly data:

log(1 + yt) = a + b·t + Σk [ αk·sin(2πkt/12) + βk·cos(2πkt/12) ]

A straight-line trend plus yearly seasonality as Fourier terms (period 12 months). Fitting in log space keeps forecasts non-negative and makes seasonality multiplicative — a plaza that doubles in size keeps the same percentage monsoon dip, which matches how toll traffic behaves.

3 · Fitting

Coefficients are estimated by ordinary least squares (normal equations, Gaussian elimination) — computed live in your browser; nothing leaves your machine. Fourier order 4 when a series has ≥24 months of history; order 2 for 12–23 months; below 12 months no forecast is produced.

4 · What the derived numbers mean

Trend growth p.a. = e12b − 1: annualized growth of the fitted trend with seasonality removed — cleaner than raw YoY, which mixes trend and calendar effects. Seasonal index = expected month ÷ average month after detrending; 1.05 means that month typically runs 5% above trend. R² is measured in log space; low values flag noisy or structurally-shifting series.

5 · Uncertainty bands

The shaded band is ±1.96σ of in-sample residuals (≈95%), widened with forecast distance by √(1 + h/24) where h is months ahead. It captures noise around the fitted curve, not uncertainty about whether the trend itself will hold.

6 · Validation

On this dataset (Jan 2023 – May 2026), a holdout backtest — fitting on all but the last 6 months and forecasting them — gave a median error (MAPE) of 8.7% across 835 plazas with ≥36 months of history; 83% landed within 20%. Errors are larger for small-revenue plazas.

7 · Data scope

This page publishes aggregate and derived metrics only: nationwide and state monthly totals, and per-plaza derived indicators (last-12-month totals, growth trends, forecast totals). Plaza-level monthly time series are not included in this page or its source. For access to detailed data, contact vivek@roadsetu.com.

8 · Limitations — read before trusting the 5-year view

The model extrapolates the fitted trend. It cannot anticipate toll rate revisions, WPI-linked resets, new parallel expressways diverting traffic, concession handovers, tolling suspensions, or decommissioning. Short horizons (6–12 months) are forecasts; the 5-year horizon is a trend-continuation scenario. Growth-leader rankings apply eligibility filters (≥24 months history, active in last 3 months, ≥₹1 cr revenue) because tiny or inactive plazas produce meaningless percentages.
